Incident Response Fundamentals

Incident response is the organized approach to addressing and managing the aftermath of a security breach or cyberattack, minimizing damage and recovery time.

Incident Response Lifecycle

  • Preparation: Establish policies, procedures, and response team
  • Identification: Detect and analyze potential incidents
  • Containment: Limit the scope and impact of the incident
  • Eradication: Remove the threat from the environment
  • Recovery: Restore systems and services to normal operation
  • Lessons Learned: Post-incident analysis and improvement

Incident Response Team

  • Incident Commander: Overall incident management
  • Security Analysts: Technical investigation and analysis
  • IT Operations: System restoration and recovery
  • Legal Counsel: Regulatory and legal compliance
  • Communications: Internal and external communications

Incident Classification

  • Low: Minor incidents with minimal impact
  • Medium: Moderate impact requiring prompt response
  • High: Significant impact requiring immediate attention
  • Critical: Severe impact with potential for major damage

Digital Forensics

Proper evidence collection and preservation are crucial for understanding the incident and potential legal proceedings:

  • Chain of custody documentation
  • Image creation and preservation
  • Timeline reconstruction
  • Malware analysis

Recovery and Business Continuity

  • System restoration procedures
  • Data recovery and validation
  • Business process resumption
  • Stakeholder communication
  • Monitoring for reoccurrence
