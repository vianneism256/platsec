Security Compliance Overview

Security compliance involves adhering to laws, regulations, and industry standards that govern how organizations should protect sensitive data and maintain security controls.

Major Compliance Frameworks

  • GDPR: General Data Protection Regulation (EU privacy law)
  • HIPAA: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act
  • PCI DSS: Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard
  • SOX: Sarbanes-Oxley Act (financial reporting)
  • FISMA: Federal Information Security Management Act

Security Standards and Frameworks

  • ISO 27001: International information security management
  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework: Risk-based approach to cybersecurity
  • CIS Controls: Critical Security Controls
  • COBIT: Control Objectives for IT and related technologies
  • OWASP: Open Web Application Security Project guidelines

Compliance Implementation

  • Gap Analysis: Identify compliance requirements vs. current state
  • Policy Development: Create and document security policies
  • Control Implementation: Deploy technical and administrative controls
  • Training and Awareness: Educate staff on compliance requirements
  • Monitoring and Auditing: Continuous compliance verification

Risk Management

  • Risk identification and assessment
  • Risk mitigation strategies
  • Risk monitoring and reporting
  • Business continuity planning
  • Disaster recovery procedures

Audit and Assessment

  • Regular internal security assessments
  • Third-party security audits
  • Compliance reporting and documentation
  • Remediation planning and tracking
  • Continuous improvement processes

Best Practices

  • Maintain current knowledge of applicable regulations
  • Implement a formal compliance program
  • Regular training and awareness programs
  • Document all compliance activities
  • Engage legal and compliance experts
